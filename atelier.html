The Atelier

Where Wood Becomes Art.

Since 2014 Kigali, Rwanda

Our Story

Born in Rwanda.
Built to Endure.

Promesse Wood Design was founded in Kigali in 2014 with a single conviction: that Rwanda's rich hardwood heritage deserved to be expressed through world-class, luxury design. What began as a small workshop with two craftsmen has grown into the region's most respected maker of fine wood furniture and kitchenware.

We work with restaurants, hotels, and private homes that share our belief that the objects we live with every day should be extraordinary — not just functional. Every Promesse piece carries the grain and character of its origin, shaped by hands that have spent years learning wood's rhythms.

We do not make furniture quickly. We make furniture that lasts a generation.

Today our team of twelve artisans works from a dedicated studio on KG 7 Avenue, Kigali — a space purpose-built for precision craftsmanship, with a seasoning room, a hand-tool workshop, and a finishing hall where every piece receives its final oils and waxes.

The Process

From Forest to Finished Piece

No shortcuts. No MDF. No machine-pressed veneer. Every stage is performed by the hands of our artisans.

01

Sourcing

We work exclusively with certified sustainable forests and licensed sawmills across Rwanda and East Africa. Each plank is hand-selected by our master craftsman for grain pattern, density, moisture content, and natural character. Only pieces that pass inspection enter our seasoning room.

02

Shaping

Every form is shaped by hand using a combination of traditional hand tools and precision joinery techniques passed down through generations of Rwandan craftsmen. We do not use CNC routers. The irregularities and subtleties in each piece are not flaws — they are the evidence of a human hand at work.

03

Finishing

Each piece is finished with a sequence of natural oils — food-safe linseed, tung, and beeswax — applied by hand in four to six coats, with a full cure period between each application. This process reveals the wood's depth of colour while protecting it from moisture and daily use for decades to come.

What We Stand For

Our Values

Craftsmanship

Every piece is made entirely by hand, from the first cut to the final oiling. We take no shortcuts and accept no compromise. Each item leaves our studio only when it meets our standard of excellence.

Sustainability

We source exclusively from certified sustainable forests. We offset our carbon through partnerships with Rwandan reforestation initiatives, and our finishing materials are entirely natural and non-toxic.

Community

Our workshop employs Rwandan artisans at fair wages, provides apprenticeship training for young craftsmen, and actively reinvests in the local woodworking tradition that makes our work possible.

The Woods We Work With

Our Material Palette

We work with a curated selection of East African and sustainably certified hardwoods, each chosen for its distinct character.

Olive Wood

Rich warm tones with dramatic swirling grain. Prized for kitchen pieces and serving boards.

Acacia

Dense and durable with warm amber hues. Ideal for dining tables and high-use restaurant furniture.

Iroko

Deep chocolate tones and exceptional hardness. A West and Central African hardwood of distinction.

Rosewood

Rare and striking, with deep reddish-brown tones. Used selectively for statement commissions only.
