Our Story

Twelve years of craft, 600 pieces made, and one simple promise — that the things you live with should be worth keeping forever.

"We believe that the objects we live and eat with every day should be extraordinary — not because they are expensive, but because someone cared enough to make them by hand."

Jean-Baptiste Habimana, Founder

Kigali studio, 2014 · Photo: Promesse Archive

The Beginning

Built from one
craftsman's promise.

In 2014, Jean-Baptiste Habimana opened a small workshop on KG 7 Ave with a single workbench, a set of chisels he had carried from Nairobi, and a promise to himself: that everything leaving this space would be made with total integrity.

His first commission was a dining table for a Kigali restaurateur who had seen him working at an open market. Word spread. By 2016, the workshop had expanded to include four artisans and a small showroom. By 2018, we had furnished the lobby of The Retreat Kigali with eight statement pieces.

Today, Promesse Wood Design operates from a 400m² studio with twelve artisans, a full design team, and clients across Rwanda, Uganda, Kenya, and beyond. The workshop is still on KG 7 Ave. The promise is unchanged.

What Drives Us

Our Values

01

Craftsmanship

Every piece passes through the hands of a master before it leaves our studio. We don't use machine-cut joints where a hand-cut joint belongs. Speed is never a reason to compromise.

02

Sustainability

We source kiln-dried hardwoods from certified suppliers across East Africa. Our finishes are water-based and food-safe. Off-cuts become bowls, boards, and handles — nothing is wasted.

03

Community

Our artisans earn living wages and participate in skills training every quarter. We partner with local vocational schools to give the next generation of Rwandan woodworkers a path into the craft.

04

Excellence

We don't cut corners. Every joint, every surface, every finish is done right — or redone until it is. If a piece doesn't meet our standard, it doesn't leave the studio. That's the promise.
